Lake Nakuru national park:

Lake Nakuru national park Lake Nakuru is famous for hosting large flocks of flamingos, which gather in the lake's alkaline waters to feed on algae. At times, the lake can be covered by a thick layer of pink as thousands of these elegant birds gather, making it one of the world's premier birdwatching destinations. While flamingos are a highlight, the park is also home to over 450 species of birds, including pelicans, herons, and various migratory birds.



Wildlife:

Lake Nakuru National Park, located in central Kenya, is renowned for its rich biodiversity and stunning landscapes. The park is famous for its large populations of flamingos that gather around the alkaline waters of Lake Nakuru, creating a spectacular sight. It is also home to a variety of wildlife, including the Big Five—lions, leopards, elephants, buffaloes, and rhinoceroses. In addition to these iconic species, the park hosts giraffes, zebras, gazelles, baboons, and many bird species, making it a popular destination for both safaris and birdwatching. The park’s diverse ecosystems, which include woodlands, grasslands, and wetlands, provide a rich habitat for its wildlife. Lake Nakuru National Park is also known for its conservation efforts, particularly for the protection of endangered black and white rhinos.
