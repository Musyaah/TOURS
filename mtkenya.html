Mount Kenya national park:

Mount Kenya national park a unique wildlife reserve located just 7 kilometers south of Nanyuki's city center in Kenya. It is the only national park in the world that lies so close to a major city. The park covers 117 square kilometers and is home to a variety of wildlife, including lions, giraffes, zebras, and over 400 species of birds. With the city skyline in the background, it offers a striking contrast between urban life and natural beauty. The park is a popular destination for safaris, offering visitors a chance to experience wildlife up close while still within the metropolitan area.



Wildlife:

Mount Kenya national park is home to a diverse range of wildlife. Among the big cats, visitors can spot lions, cheetahs, and leopards, though they are not as numerous as in other parks. The park also has a variety of herbivores, including zebras, giraffes, buffaloes, and several antelope species such as Grant's gazelles and impalas. For bird lovers, the park is a haven, with over 400 species of birds, including eagles, ostriches, flamingos, and various waterfowl, especially around the park's wetlands. Additionally, the park is home to a range of other mammals such as warthogs, hyenas, baboons, and monkeys, creating a vibrant and dynamic ecosystem to explore.
